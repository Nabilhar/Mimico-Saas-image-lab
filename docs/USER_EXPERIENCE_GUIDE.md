## What is Shoreline Studio?

**Shoreline Studio** is an AI content creation platform designed specifically for local businesses. It helps you:

✨ **Generate authentic social media posts** in seconds  
🎨 **Create matching images** that reflect your brand  
📍 **Infuse local context** into your content  
🎯 **Maintain consistent voice** across all posts  
⚡ **Save hours** of content creation time  

### What Makes Shoreline Studio Different?

**1. Location-Aware Content**  
Unlike generic AI writers, Shoreline Studio understands your neighborhood. It knows your local landmarks, transit stations, and community context — making your posts feel genuinely local, not cookie-cutter.

**2. Brand Intelligence**  
The platform learns your visual identity (colors, style, atmosphere) and craft identity (what makes you unique, your products/services, your approach). Every post reflects YOUR brand, not a template.

**3. Cognitive Patterns**  
Instead of generic "tips and tricks," Shoreline uses **cognitive lenses** — patterns of insight that experts naturally use when talking about their craft. This makes your content feel authentic and knowledgeable, not like AI-generated filler.

**4. Real-World Aesthetics**  
Generated images look like actual photos from your business — not polished stock photos or obvious AI art. Flour dust on the counter, natural lighting, authentic workspace details.

## Understanding Brand Discovery

**Brand Discovery** is Shoreline's process of learning about your business. It happens once (with your first profile setup) and can be re-run if your business changes significantly.

**🎨 Visual Identity Learning (Always Free)**
- Analyzes photos of your business
- Extracts your color palette (primary, secondary, accent)
- Identifies architectural style (storefront appearance)
- Understands interior design (layout, lighting, materials)
- Recognizes logo colors

**🔍 Craft Identity Research (First Free, Then 3 Credits)**
- Researches your business online
- Discovers what makes you unique
- Identifies your products and services
- Learns your neighborhood context (landmarks, transit)
- Finds local trends (customer behaviors)
- Optionally discovers contact info (social media, website)

### How to Use Brand Discovery

#### Option 1: Photos Only (Recommended for First Setup)

**Upload 1-3 photos:**
- **Storefront:** Front exterior showing signage, entrance, materials
- **Logo:** Close-up of your logo (if visible on storefront, menu, packaging)
- **Interior:** Workspace, seating area, or representative room

**Tips for Good Photos:**
- Use natural lighting (not flash)
- Take photos during business hours
- Show real workspace (not staged)
- Include people working (optional but authentic)
- Don't worry about perfect framing — authenticity > polish

**Cost:** **Free**

**What Happens:**
1. You upload photos
2. Shoreline analyzes them (takes 30-60 seconds)
3. Your color theme and visual style are saved
4. You can start generating content immediately

#### Option 2: Photos + Text Research (Most Comprehensive)

**Check the box: "🔍 Re-run Brand Discovery"**

**Cost:** 
- **First time:** **FREE** (your welcome gift!)
- **After that:** **3 credits** each time

**What Happens:**
1. Shoreline searches the web for your business
2. Finds your website, social media, reviews, listings
3. Extracts information about:
   - What you're known for
   - Your craft approach
   - Your products/services
   - Your neighborhood
   - Local customer behaviors
4. Combines this with photo analysis (if you uploaded photos)
5. Creates a comprehensive brand profile

**When to Use:**
- ✅ First time setting up
- ✅ Changed business name
- ✅ Moved locations
- ✅ Major rebrand (new logo, colors, focus)

**When NOT to Use:**
- ❌ Just changed voice/tone (doesn't need re-research)

**Cost:** First time free, then 3 credits

## Understanding Post Modes

Shoreline uses **6 post modes** — each with a distinct structure and purpose. Think of them as different "formats" you can use to communicate.

### 📚 Mode 1: Tip of the Day (EDUCATION)

**Purpose:** Share 3 practical tips

**Structure:**
```
Context setup (1-2 sentences)

1. [First tip with explanation]
2. [Second tip with explanation]
3. [Third tip with explanation]

Optional closing line
```

**Example (Bakery):**
> "Three things that determine whether your sourdough rises properly at home:
>
> 1. Water temperature. 78-82°F activates the starter without shocking the culture. Colder water slows fermentation; hotter water kills it.
>
> 2. Flour protein content. Bread flour (12-14% protein) builds the gluten network that traps gas. All-purpose flour (10-12%) makes a denser loaf.
>
> 3. Ambient humidity. Dry air forms a crust on the dough surface before it rises. Cover with damp towel or proof in closed oven with bowl of water.
>
> These aren't optional. They're the three variables that determine whether you get rise or disappointment."

**When to Use:**
- Sharing practical knowledge
- Teaching your craft
- Establishing expertise

**Best For:** Health & Wellness, Home Services, Professional Services

---

### ❌ Mode 2: Myth-busting (EDUCATION)

**Purpose:** Correct one specific misconception

**Structure:**
```
State the myth as fact (how people think about it)

Show what actually happens instead (observable reality)

State the correction (what's true in practice)
```

**Example (Physiotherapy):**
> "People think stretching a tight muscle makes it longer and more flexible.
>
> But the tissue doesn't actually change length. The tightness you feel is your nervous system bracing — usually because it's compensating for instability somewhere else. You stretch the hamstring, it relaxes temporarily, then the nervous system tightens it again because the hip instability hasn't changed.
>
> We address the instability first. The tightness resolves when the nervous system stops perceiving threat."

**When to Use:**
- Correcting widespread beliefs
- Explaining counterintuitive truths
- Addressing customer confusion

**Best For:** Health & Wellness, Home Services, Technical fields

---

### 🎬 Mode 3: Behind the Scenes (OBSERVATION)

**Purpose:** Show a specific moment in your process

**Structure:**
```
Present-tense observation of what's happening right now

Why this moment matters / what it determines

(No CTA, no sales pitch — just the moment)
```

**Example (HVAC Contractor):**
> "The technician is on the roof at 6:47 AM, measuring the condenser pad before the day heats up. The pad is level within 1/8 inch — any more tilt and the compressor oil pools wrong, which shortens the unit's lifespan by years.
>
> By 8 AM, the sun will have expanded the roofing material enough to shift the pad. This narrow window between cooldown and heatup is when you get accurate readings and make adjustments that hold.
>
> Most installations happen midday. That's why so many condensers fail early."

**When to Use:**
- Showing your process
- Making invisible work visible
- Building appreciation for craft

**Best For:** All categories, especially Home Services, Food & Beverage

---

### 🎁 Mode 4: Promotion / Offer (OBSERVATION)

**Purpose:** Announce a deal without sales pressure

**Structure:**
```
Atmospheric description of the offer

Factual details (what, when, how)

(No urgency tactics, no CTA — just information)
```

**Example (Cafe):**
> "Friday mornings now include a warm cheddar scone with any coffee order. The scones come out of the oven at 7:15 AM — flaky outside, soft inside, aged cheddar folded into the dough.
>
> Available until noon or until we run out. No coupon needed. Just mention it when you order.
>
> Started last week. Sold out both days by 10:30."

**When to Use:**
- Announcing specials
- Promoting new products
- Sharing limited offers

**Best For:** Food & Beverage, Retail, Personal Services

---

### 📍 Mode 5: Local Event / Shout-out (CASUAL)

**Purpose:** Connect with your community

**Structure:**
```
Casual mention of local event, business, or happening

Why it matters or what's notable

Optional invitation or connection
```

**Example (Bookstore):**
> "The vintage market at Christie Pits runs this Saturday, 9 AM to 3 PM. About 40 vendors — mostly furniture, records, and estate jewelry.
>
> We're setting up a table near the north entrance with overstock hardcovers, $5 each. Fiction, history, some art books.
>
> Weather looks clear. Bring cash — half the vendors don't take cards."

**When to Use:**
- Highlighting local events
- Supporting other businesses
- Engaging with neighborhood

**Best For:** All categories, especially Community-Focused voice

---

### 🌅 Mode 6: Community Moment (ATMOSPHERIC)

**Purpose:** Create ambient presence (no teaching, no selling)

**Structure:**
```
Pure sensory observation of a moment

Atmosphere, sounds, sights, textures

(No explanation, no significance — just the moment)
```

**Example (Yoga Studio):**
> "Friday evening, 6:15 PM. The late light comes through the west windows in soft gold bars across the floor. Fifteen mats are laid out, each person finding their spot in the quiet before class starts.
>
> The room smells faintly of sandalwood. Someone's adjusting a bolster, another rolling out their shoulders. The city hum is there but distant — the space holds a different rhythm.
>
> In a few minutes the class begins, but right now it's just this: the in-between, the settling, the day loosening its grip."

**When to Use:**
- Creating atmosphere
- Building ambient presence
- No specific message to deliver

**Best For:** Personal Care, Wellness, Creative spaces

## Working with Images

Shoreline can generate photorealistic images to accompany your posts. These aren't stock photos or obvious AI art — they're designed to look like **actual photos from your business**.

### How Image Generation Works

**The Architect:**
When you enable images, a specialized AI component called the "Architect" analyzes your post and brand data to write a detailed image prompt. It considers:

- Your post's message
- Your color palette
- Your storefront/interior style
- Your craft's visual elements
- Authentic workspace details

**Example Architect Prompt:**
> "Medium shot of physiotherapist's hands guiding patient's shoulder through passive range of motion test. Natural clinic lighting from window. Clinical assessment table visible, posture chart in background. Clean professional space, blue-gray wall tones matching clinic brand. Focus on hand placement and controlled movement. Authentic healthcare workspace, real examination moment."

**The Generator:**
This prompt goes to Gemini Imagen 3, which creates a 1024×1024 photorealistic image.

### What Makes Shoreline Images Different

**❌ NOT This (Generic Stock Photos):**
- Perfect lighting, staged scene
- Models posing awkwardly
- Fake "candid" moments
- Obvious studio setup
- No brand personality

**✅ YES This (Authentic Workspace):**
- Natural lighting (window light, workspace lamps)
- Real work in progress
- Authentic details (flour dust, tools in use, actual materials)
- Your color palette integrated naturally
- Feels like someone pulled out their phone and captured the moment

### Tips for Better Images

**1. Run Brand Discovery First**
Images will be generic if Shoreline doesn't know your visual identity. Upload photos during profile setup!

**2. Check the Generated Image**
Sometimes AI makes mistakes:
- Wrong colors
- Unrealistic details
- Doesn't match your business

If the image is off, **regenerate the post** or **just save the text** and create your own image.

**3. Use Images Strategically**
You don't need an image for every post. Consider:
- Behind the scenes → Great with image
- Myth-busting → Usually better text-only
- Community moment → Perfect with image
- Tip of the Day → Text-only often clearer

**4. Download in High Quality**
Images are 1024×1024 — perfect for Instagram square posts. For Facebook/LinkedIn, they'll auto-crop to fit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is the content really written by AI?**  
Yes. Shoreline uses Claude Haiku 4.5 (from Anthropic) for text generation and Gemini Imagen 3 (from Google) for images. However, the AI is guided by sophisticated prompts that include your brand data, location context, and craft patterns — so it doesn't sound generic.

**Q: Can I use the generated content anywhere?**  
Yes! You own the content. Use it on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, your website, email newsletters, printed materials — anywhere you'd use marketing content.

**Q: Will my posts sound robotic or obviously AI-written?**  
No. Shoreline is specifically designed to avoid "AI voice" (generic, overly enthusiastic, buzzword-heavy). The cognitive lens system creates posts that sound like they come from someone who genuinely knows the craft.

**Q: How is this different from ChatGPT?**  
ChatGPT is a general-purpose assistant. Shoreline is purpose-built for local business social content with:
- Brand discovery (learns your visual + craft identity)
- Location awareness (neighborhood context)
- Cognitive lens patterns (authentic expert voice)
- Image generation (matching your aesthetic)
- Optimized for social media (length, tone, structure)
