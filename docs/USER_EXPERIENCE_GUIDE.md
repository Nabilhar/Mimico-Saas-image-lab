## What is Shoreline Studio?

**Shoreline Studio** is an AI content creation platform designed specifically for local businesses. It helps you:

✨ **Generate authentic social media posts** in seconds  
🎨 **Create matching images** that reflect your brand  
📍 **Infuse local context** into your content  
🎯 **Maintain consistent voice** across all posts  
⚡ **Save hours** of content creation time  

### What Makes Shoreline Studio Different?

**1. Location-Aware Content**  
Unlike generic AI writers, Shoreline Studio understands your neighborhood. It knows your local landmarks, transit stations, and community context — making your posts feel genuinely local, not cookie-cutter.

**2. Brand Intelligence**  
The platform learns your visual identity (colors, style, atmosphere per zone of your space) and craft identity (your specific offerings, signature practices, what makes you unique). Every post reflects YOUR brand, not a template.

**3. Built-In Content Variety**  
Each post type has its own rotation of sub-types so your content never covers the same angle twice in a row. Tips cycle through: Technique, Timing, Selection, Care, Tools/setup, and more. Behind the scenes rotates between: Early prep, Active craft, The adjustment, The standard, and others. The system tracks what you've used recently and automatically picks what you haven't.

**4. Offering Rotation**  
Shoreline tracks which of your services or products has been featured recently and automatically rotates to a different one each time. If you have three offerings, your posts naturally cycle through all of them — no more every post sounding like it's about the same thing.

**5. Real-World Aesthetics**  
Generated images look like actual photos from your business — not polished stock photos or obvious AI art. Flour dust on the counter, natural lighting, authentic workspace details.

## Understanding Brand Discovery

**Brand Discovery** is Shoreline's process of learning about your business. It runs once during your first profile setup and can be re-run whenever your business changes significantly.

### What Brand Discovery Does

**🎨 Visual Identity Learning — Your Space in Three Zones (Always Free)**

Shoreline now analyzes your space in three distinct zones:

- **Storefront/Patio** — Street-level view — skip if your business has no outdoor presence (e.g. upper-floor offices, indoor-only units)
- **Customer Space** — Where the customer experience happens: seating area, service counter, retail floor
- **Work Space** — Where the craft is performed: kitchen, treatment room, workshop, studio

For each zone, Shoreline extracts:
- Color palette (dominant, supporting, accent, surface descriptions)
- Layout (how the space is organized, focal features, materials)
- Lighting and atmosphere
- What activity happens there

This zone structure helps generate images that are spatially accurate — a "behind the scenes" post gets the work space aesthetic, a "community moment" post gets the customer space feeling.

**🔍 Craft Identity Research — Your Offerings & Practices (First Free, Then 3 Credits)**

Shoreline researches your business online and discovers:
- What makes you unique (your identity story)
- **Your specific offerings** and the signature practices behind each one
  - Example for a yoga studio: "Vinyasa Yoga" → ["60-minute heated flow at 85°F", "Max 25 mats per class", "All-levels with modifications offered every pose"]
  - Example for a bakery: "Sourdough Bread" → ["48-hour cold fermentation", "Scored by hand before baking", "Seasonal levain adjustments weekly"]
- Your neighborhood (landmarks, transit, local customer patterns)
- Contact information (email, social handles)

**Why offerings matter:** These specific practices feed directly into your post content. Instead of generic tips, Shoreline can write "The fermentation window that determines your crumb structure" — specific, real, and accurate to your actual methods.

### How to Upload Photos

The profile page has three labeled photo slots:

| Slot | Label | What to photograph |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| **Storefront / Patio** | Street-level view |  skip if your business has no outdoor presence (e.g. upper-floor offices, indoor-only units) |
| **Customer Space** | Interior | Seating area, service counter, retail floor |
| **Work Space** | Behind the scenes | Kitchen, treatment room, prep area, workshop |
*Businesses without street-level presence (3rd-floor offices, mall units) should skip the Storefront slot. Upload Customer Space and Work Space only — image generation will use the customer area when a neighbourhood post calls for an exterior scene.

**Tips for Good Photos:**
- Use natural lighting (not flash)
- Take during business hours
- Show real workspace — not staged
- People working are fine and add authenticity
- Don't worry about framing perfection

## Understanding Post Modes

### 📚 Mode 1: Tip of the Day (EDUCATION)

**Purpose:** Share 3 practical tips from your craft

**What's different with offering rotation:** Shoreline selects ONE of your offerings to focus on each time. If you have "Cut," "Color," and "Treatment" services, the tips rotate through each over time — so you're not always writing about the same service.

**Structure:**
```
Context setup (1-2 sentences referencing your offering)

1. [First tip with explanation]
2. [Second tip with explanation]
3. [Third tip with explanation]

Optional closing line
```

Scene types:
- Technique — how a specific method is executed
- Timing — when to do something, or for how long
- Selection — how to choose between options (materials, cuts, products)
- Care / storage — how to preserve quality after the work is done
- Pairing / combining — what works together and why
- The why — the reasoning behind a practice most people skip
- Tools / setup — how the right equipment changes the outcome

Image behaviour:
All scene types → same visual job: show the result of the technique 
or the setup that enables it.

Product businesses (food, retail, trades): the technique's finished 
result placed in the work space — the crumb structure, the finished 
repair, the completed arrangement.

Experience businesses (wellness, fitness, beauty): the tool or 
setup the technique uses — blocks on a mat, scissors and clips 
laid out at the station, equipment positioned correctly.

**Example (Hair Salon — Precision Cuts offering):**
> "The dry-cut technique changes what you can see — and correct — during the cut.
>
> 1. Natural growth pattern. Dry hair falls where it actually lives, not where water pulls it. We start there to read the real structure before anything else.
>
> 2. The first cut is structural. Ninety minutes on a new client isn't about being slow — it's a consultation happening simultaneously with the cut. The bone structure assessment shapes every decision.
>
> 3. The perimeter sets everything. Once the length line is established dry, the layers and texture fall relative to it. Change the perimeter last and you change the whole balance."

**When to Use:** Sharing practical knowledge, establishing expertise  
**Best For:** Health & Wellness, Home Services, Professional Services

---

### 🎬 Mode 2: Behind the Scenes (OBSERVATION)

**Purpose:** Show a specific moment from your process

**Structure:**
```
Present-tense observation of what's happening right now
Why this moment matters / what it determines
(No CTA, no sales pitch — just the moment)
```

Scene types:
- Early prep — what happens before the doors open or the session starts
- Active craft — the main work happening in real time
- Wait / passive time — the part of the process where time does the work 
  (fermentation, curing, a long-hold stretch)
- The adjustment — a micro-correction that changes the outcome
- Repetition — the part that only gets right through accumulated doing
- The standard — a baseline practice that defines the quality level

Image behaviour:
All scene types → same visual job: show what the described moment 
produces or prepares, not the moment itself.

Product businesses: the finished output that the process creates — 
the loaf on the rack, the car off the lift, the arrangement complete.

Experience businesses: the workspace prepared for delivery — mat and 
props positioned, station set, tools laid out with craft intent.

**Example (HVAC Contractor):**
> "The technician is on the roof at 6:47 AM, measuring the condenser pad before the day heats up. The pad is level within 1/8 inch — any more tilt and the compressor oil pools wrong, which shortens the unit's lifespan by years.
>
> By 8 AM, the sun will have expanded the roofing material enough to shift the pad. This narrow window between cooldown and heatup is when you get accurate readings and make adjustments that hold.
>
> Most installations happen midday. That's why so many condensers fail early."

**When to Use:** Showing your process, making invisible work visible  
**Best For:** All categories, especially Home Services, Food & Beverage

### 🎁 Mode 3: Promotion / Offer (OBSERVATION)

**Purpose:** Announce a deal without sales pressure

**Structure:**
```
Atmospheric description of the offer
Factual details (what, when, how)

```

No scene type rotation — this mode is content-driven by the offer

Image behaviour:

Product businesses: the specific product assembled and ready — on the

Experience businesses: the service environment set for the experience 
the offer promises — treatment room ready, studio set, station prepared.

**Example (Cafe):**
> "Friday mornings now include a warm cheddar scone with any coffee order. The scones come out of the oven at 7:15 AM — flaky outside, soft inside, aged cheddar folded into the dough.
>
> Available until noon or until we run out. No coupon needed. Just mention it when you order.
>
> Started last week. Sold out both days by 10:30."

**When to Use:** Announcing specials, promoting new products, sharing limited offers  
**Best For:** Food & Beverage, Retail, Personal Services

### 📍 Mode 4: Local Event / News (CASUAL)

**Purpose:** Connect with your community

**Structure:**
```
Casual mention of local event, business, or happening
Why it matters or what's notable
Optional invitation or connection
```

Scene types for Local Event / News:
- Neighborhood rhythm — what your block does as a pattern
- Season / weather impact — how conditions affect your day or space  
- The regular — a recurring customer tied to a local habit
- Peak / rush — a busy moment linked to something happening nearby
- Shared moment — your space participating in a community event

Image behaviour:
Neighborhood rhythm and Season / weather impact → image shows your 
storefront or patio in its seasonal moment, with street-level context.

The regular, Peak / rush, Shared moment → image shows the inside 
customer space when the scene is happening — similar to Community Moment.

**Example (Bookstore):**
> "The vintage market at Christie Pits runs this Saturday, 9 AM to 3 PM. About 40 vendors — mostly furniture, records, and estate jewelry.
>
> We're setting up a table near the north entrance with overstock hardcovers, $5 each. Fiction, history, some art books.
>
> Weather looks clear. Bring cash — half the vendors don't take cards."

**When to Use:** Highlighting local events, supporting neighboring businesses  
**Best For:** All categories, especially Community-Focused voice

---

### 🌅 Mode 5: Community Moment (ATMOSPHERIC)

**Purpose:** Create ambient presence — no teaching, no selling

**Structure:**
```
Pure sensory observation of a moment
Atmosphere, sounds, sights, textures
(No explanation, no significance — just the moment)
```

Scene types:
- Peak / rush — the space at its fullest, most active moment
- Lull / quiet — the in-between: after the rush, before the next wave
- Solo customer — one person, one corner, one ritual
- Shared moment — multiple people in the same space, each in their own world
- Weather-shaped — how rain, heat, the first cold day changes how 
  the space feels and who comes in

Image behaviour:
All scene types → same zone (customer space), same visual job: show 
the room when that scene is happening.

Peak / rush → wider composition, activity evidence spread across the space.
Lull / quiet → medium shot, a single corner or surface, stillness visible.
Solo customer → intimate medium shot, one spot with evidence of one person.
Shared moment → wider, multiple figures as atmosphere, each in their own orbit.
Weather-shaped → window light prominent, weather mood visible through glass 
or in how the space is lit.

People are always atmosphere — blurred, partial, angled away, never identifiable.

**Example (Yoga Studio):**
> "Friday evening, 6:15 PM. The late light comes through the west windows in soft gold bars across the floor. Fifteen mats are laid out, each person finding their spot in the quiet before class starts.
>
> The room smells faintly of sandalwood. Someone's adjusting a bolster, another rolling out their shoulders. The city hum is there but distant — the space holds a different rhythm.
>
> In a few minutes the class begins, but right now it's just this: the in-between, the settling, the day loosening its grip."

**When to Use:** Creating atmosphere, building ambient presence  
**Best For:** Personal Care, Wellness, Creative spaces

## Working with Images

Shoreline can generate photorealistic images to accompany your posts. These are designed to look like **actual photos from your business** — not stock photos or obvious AI art.

### How Image Generation Works

**1. Save your post first.**

You must save the post before generating an image. The save step creates the post record that the image system reads from.

**2. Click "Generate Image."**

Shoreline selects the right zone photo from your brand discovery (entrance, customer space, or work space — depending on your post type) and sends it to Gemini's image model alongside a structured prompt describing the scene.

**3. Your Real Space, AI-Extended**

This is what makes Shoreline images different from generic AI generators. Instead of inventing a space from scratch, Gemini takes your actual uploaded photo as the reference setting and generates a new image that:
- Matches the exact lighting, materials, and color temperature of your real space
- Places the post's subject (the craft moment, the atmospheric detail) naturally inside it
- Feels like a professional photo taken in your actual business

The zone selection is post-type-aware:
- **Behind the scenes** → uses your work space photo
- **Community moment / Promotion / Local event/news** → uses your customer space or storefront/patio photo
- **Tips** → uses your work space photo (detail shot focus)


**4. Fallback: Architect + FLUX**

If you haven't uploaded a zone photo for the relevant zone, the system falls back to the Architect path — a text-based image prompt is written and sent to the FLUX image chain (DeepInfra → OpenRouter → HuggingFace → Cloudflare). The image is not brand-aware, it's infered instead of anchored in your real space.

**Takes 15-45 seconds.**

**5. Credits deducted on success.**

3 credits are deducted only when a successful image is returned and saved. If generation fails, no credits are charged.

### What Makes Shoreline Images Different

**❌ NOT This (Generic Stock Photos):**
- Perfect lighting, staged scene
- Models posing awkwardly
- Obvious "candid" shots
- No brand personality

**✅ YES This (Authentic Workspace):**
- Natural lighting (window light, workspace lamps)
- Real work in progress
- Authentic details (flour dust, tools in use, actual surfaces from your space)
- Your color palette integrated naturally
- Feels like someone pulled out their phone during a real moment

### Tips for Better Images

**1. Run Brand Discovery with Photos**

Images will be generic if Shoreline doesn't know your visual identity. Upload zone photos during profile setup for the best results.

**2. Check the Generated Image**

Sometimes AI makes mistakes:
- Wrong colors
- Unrealistic details
- Doesn't match your business type

If the image is off, save the text post only and use your own photo instead.

**3. Use Images Strategically**

Not every post needs an image. Consider:
- Behind the scenes → Great with image
- Community moment → Perfect with image
- Tip of the Day → Text-only often clearer

**4. Upload Zone Photos for Best Results**

The more zone photos you have uploaded (Storefront/patio, customer space, work space), the more accurately the system can anchor images in your real space. Without zone photos, the system falls back to text-based generation which is still good but not grounded in your actual location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is the content really written by AI?**  
Yes. Shoreline uses Claude Haiku 4.5 (from Anthropic) for text generation and gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview for images. However, the AI is guided by sophisticated prompts that include your specific offerings, zone-aware brand data, location context, and craft patterns — so it doesn't sound generic.

**Q: Can I use the generated content anywhere?**  
Yes! You own the content. Use it on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, your website, email newsletters, printed materials — anywhere.

**Q: Will my posts sound robotic or obviously AI-written?**  
No. Shoreline is specifically designed to avoid "AI voice" (generic, overly enthusiastic, buzzword-heavy). The cognitive lens system creates posts that sound like they come from someone who genuinely knows the craft.

**Q: How is this different from ChatGPT?**  
ChatGPT is a general-purpose assistant. Shoreline is purpose-built for local business social content with:
- Brand discovery (learns your specific offerings, visual zones, craft identity)
- Location awareness (neighborhood context)
- New topics for every post

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Posts feel too generic

**Symptoms:** Content could apply to any business, no specific examples from your craft, lacks local context

**Solutions:**
1. ✅ **Run brand discovery with text research** — Go to Profile → Check "Re-run Brand Discovery" → Save
2. ✅ **Use a more specific niche** — "Restaurant" → "Farm-to-Table Bistro"
3. ✅ **Upload zone photos** — Visual context shapes content
4. ✅ **Try a different post type** — "Behind the Scenes" and "Myth-busting" are most specific

---

### Problem: Posts always reference the same offering

**Symptoms:** Every tip is about the same service, posts feel repetitive

**Solutions:**
1. ✅ **Re-run brand discovery with text research** — The research needs to find all your offerings with their specific practices
2. ✅ **Check your niche is specific** — Generic niches produce generic offering lists
3. ✅ **Generate more posts** — The rotation needs at least 2-3 posts in the library to have history to rotate from

---

### Problem: Images don't match my brand

**Symptoms:** Wrong colors, generic stock-photo feel, doesn't look like my space

**Solutions:**
1. ✅ **Upload zone photos for brand discovery** — Entrance, Customer Space, Work Space
2. ✅ **Use well-lit photos** — Natural light works best; avoid dark or blurry shots
3. ✅ **Re-run brand discovery with photos** — Profile → Upload new zone photos → Save
4. ✅ **Try generating again** — The Architect writes a new prompt each time
